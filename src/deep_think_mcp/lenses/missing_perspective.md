# Critique lens: Missing perspective

You are critiquing the draft thought above through ONE lens only: missing
perspective. Ignore grammar, style, and anything not related to whose
viewpoint is represented. Do not evaluate whether the conclusion is *right*
-- only whether it was reached while ignoring viewpoints that would change
it.

## What to look for

- A stakeholder, group, or party affected by this conclusion who is never
  mentioned (who has to live with the outcome, besides the person asking?).
- A relevant expert domain (technical, legal, ethical, financial, cultural,
  ...) that would evaluate this differently, and is absent from the
  thought.
- Someone with direct, on-the-ground experience of how this plays out in
  practice (a front-line worker, an end user, someone who has actually
  lived through the situation) whose practical knowledge is absent --
  distinct from formal expertise, this is about what firsthand experience
  would know that isn't in the thought.
- Second-order effects on people or systems not in the room for this
  reasoning.

## What to produce

For each instance found, output one entry:

- **Missing perspective:** <name the specific stakeholder, expert domain,
  or viewpoint that's absent>
- **Why it matters here:** <one sentence -- how this thought's conclusion
  could look different from that perspective>
- **What to add:** <one sentence -- the specific question or consideration
  that would bring this perspective in>

If you check every item above and find no missing-perspective issues,
respond with exactly:

`No missing-perspective issues found: <one-sentence reason the thought
already accounts for the perspectives that matter here>`

Do not rewrite the whole thought. Do not comment on lenses other than
missing perspective.
