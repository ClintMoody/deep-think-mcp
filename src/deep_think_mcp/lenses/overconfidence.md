# Critique lens: Overconfidence

You are critiquing the draft thought above through ONE lens only:
overconfidence. Ignore grammar, style, and anything not related to
unjustified certainty. Do not evaluate whether the conclusion is *right* --
only whether it is stated with *more certainty than it has earned*.

## What to look for

- Absolute words (`always`, `never`, `guaranteed`, `definitely`, `obviously`,
  `clearly`, `the only way`) attached to a claim that isn't actually
  universal or self-evident.
- A conclusion, prediction, or recommendation stated flatly with no
  confidence level, caveat, or acknowledged failure mode.
- A claim whose certainty outruns the evidence given for it elsewhere in the
  thought (e.g. one data point backing a sweeping claim).
- No mention of what would have to be true for the conclusion to be wrong,
  or of any scenario where it doesn't hold.

## What to produce

For each instance found, output one entry:

- **Overconfident claim:** <quote the exact sentence or phrase>
- **Why it's overconfident:** <one sentence -- what certainty it claims vs.
  what's actually supported>
- **Better framing:** <one sentence rewrite that states the same idea at an
  honest confidence level, e.g. adding a caveat, a probability, or a named
  exception>

If you check every item above and find no overconfident claims, respond
with exactly:

`No overconfidence issues found: <one-sentence reason the thought's
certainty level matches its evidence>`

Do not rewrite the whole thought. Do not comment on lenses other than
overconfidence.
