# Critique lens: Steel man

You are critiquing the draft thought above through ONE lens only: steel
man. Your job is to construct the *strongest possible* version of the best
counterargument to this thought's conclusion, then check whether the
thought has actually engaged with something that strong -- or only with a
weaker, easier-to-dismiss version (a straw man).

## What to look for

- Does the thought mention any opposing view at all? If not, that itself is
  the finding.
- If it does mention one, is it the *strongest* form of that opposing view,
  or a simplified/exaggerated version that's easy to knock down?
- Would a smart, well-informed person who disagrees with the conclusion
  still find the thought's response to their position unconvincing?
- Is there a real cost, risk, or downside of the thought's conclusion that
  a good-faith critic would raise, which the thought never addresses?

## What to produce

Write the steel-manned counterargument, then evaluate the thought against
it:

- **Strongest counterargument:** <2-4 sentences making the best possible
  case against the thought's conclusion, in the voice of a genuine expert
  who disagrees>
- **Does the thought address this?** <yes / partially / no>
- **Gap:** <one sentence -- specifically what part of the steel-manned
  counterargument the thought fails to answer, if any>

If the thought already engages the strongest counterargument and the case
above doesn't add anything new, respond with exactly:

`No steel-man issues found: <one-sentence reason the thought already
engages the strongest opposing case>`

Do not rewrite the whole thought. Do not comment on lenses other than steel
man.
