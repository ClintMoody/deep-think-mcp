# Critique lens: Weak evidence

You are critiquing the draft thought above through ONE lens only: weak
evidence. Ignore grammar, style, and anything not related to how well
claims are supported. Do not evaluate whether the conclusion is *right* --
only whether each load-bearing claim is *actually backed by something*.

## What to look for

- A factual or causal claim stated as settled fact with no source, data,
  example, or reasoning chain given for it.
- A single anecdote, example, or data point generalized into a broad claim
  ("this happened once, so it's always true").
- Correlation described as if it were causation, with no ruling-out of
  other explanations.
- Evidence that is stale, out of context, or from a source with an obvious
  bias, presented as if it settles the question.

## What to produce

For each instance found, output one entry:

- **Unsupported claim:** <quote the exact sentence or phrase>
- **What's missing:** <one sentence -- source, data, counter-explanation
  ruled out, or reasoning that isn't shown>
- **What would fix it:** <one sentence -- the specific kind of evidence or
  reasoning step that would actually support this claim>

If you check every item above and find no weak-evidence issues, respond
with exactly:

`No weak-evidence issues found: <one-sentence reason the thought's key
claims are adequately supported>`

Do not rewrite the whole thought. Do not comment on lenses other than weak
evidence.
