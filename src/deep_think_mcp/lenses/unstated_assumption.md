# Critique lens: Unstated assumption

You are critiquing the draft thought above through ONE lens only: unstated
assumption. Ignore grammar, style, and anything not related to hidden
premises. Do not evaluate whether the conclusion is *right* -- only whether
it silently depends on something that was never said out loud.

## What to look for

- A conclusion that only follows if some unstated premise is true -- ask
  "if this premise were false, would the reasoning collapse?"
- A key term used without being defined (e.g. "better", "fast", "safe",
  "scalable") where the definition chosen quietly decides the outcome.
- Context taken for granted (budget, timeline, audience, environment,
  constraints) that was never confirmed, only assumed.
- A priority or trade-off assumed without being named (e.g. assuming speed
  matters more than cost, or that convenience outweighs risk) -- the
  thought never states it's making that trade-off, but the conclusion
  flips if the priority is reversed.

## What to produce

For each instance found, output one entry:

- **Unstated assumption:** <state the hidden premise in one sentence, as if
  someone had to say it out loud>
- **Where it's load-bearing:** <quote or point to the part of the thought
  that depends on it>
- **What to check:** <one sentence -- the question that would confirm or
  break this assumption>

If you check every item above and find no unstated assumptions, respond
with exactly:

`No unstated-assumption issues found: <one-sentence reason the thought's
premises are all explicit>`

Do not rewrite the whole thought. Do not comment on lenses other than
unstated assumption.
