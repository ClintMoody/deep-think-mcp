# Critique lens: Alternative framing

You are critiquing the draft thought above through ONE lens only:
alternative framing. Ignore grammar, style, and anything not related to
whether a different way of framing the problem would change the answer. Do
not evaluate whether the conclusion is *right* -- only whether it's the
product of the *only* framing considered.

## What to look for

- The thought treats one definition or framing of the problem as the only
  possible one, with no acknowledgment that another framing exists.
- A false dichotomy: the thought presents two options as exhaustive when a
  third (or a spectrum) is available.
- Reframing the goal along a different axis (cost vs. risk, short-term vs.
  long-term, individual vs. system, quality vs. speed) would plausibly
  change which answer looks best, and that axis is never raised.
- The question could be restated at a different level (more literal, more
  abstract, inverted) in a way that leads somewhere the thought never goes.

## What to produce

For each instance found, output one entry:

- **Framing used:** <one sentence -- the framing the thought committed to>
- **Alternative framing:** <one sentence -- a genuinely different way to
  frame the same problem>
- **How the answer might change:** <one sentence -- what conclusion the
  alternative framing points toward instead>

If you check every item above and find no alternative-framing issues,
respond with exactly:

`No alternative-framing issues found: <one-sentence reason the framing used
is the right one, having genuinely considered at least one alternative>`

Do not rewrite the whole thought. Do not comment on lenses other than
alternative framing.
