# Critique lens: Scope creep

You are critiquing the draft thought above through ONE lens only: scope
creep. Ignore grammar, style, and anything not related to whether the
thought stayed on the question it was actually asked. Do not evaluate
whether the conclusion is *right* -- only whether it answers something
bigger, smaller, or different from what was asked.

## What to look for

- The thought quietly answers a broader question than was asked (solving
  the whole category of problem instead of this instance of it).
- The thought narrows the question without saying so, dodging the hard part
  by answering an easier, adjacent version of it.
- A new goal, requirement, or constraint appears mid-thought that wasn't
  part of the original question and isn't justified.
- Effort spent on a sub-problem that isn't actually necessary to answer the
  original question, at the expense of the parts that are.

## What to produce

For each instance found, output one entry:

- **Scope drift:** <quote the part of the thought where it happens>
- **Original vs. actual scope:** <one sentence -- what was asked vs. what
  is actually being answered here>
- **How to refocus:** <one sentence -- what to cut or narrow to get back to
  the original question>

If you check every item above and find no scope-creep issues, respond with
exactly:

`No scope-creep issues found: <one-sentence reason the thought stays
matched to the original question>`

Do not rewrite the whole thought. Do not comment on lenses other than scope
creep.
